## 9. Optimization

Maximize:

$$
A = xy
$$

Given:

$$
y = 3 - x^2
$$

### Step 1: Express as single variable

$$
A(x) = x(3 - x^2)
$$

$$
A(x) = 3x - x^3
$$

### Step 2: First derivative

$$
A'(x) = 3 - 3x^2
$$

### Step 3: Critical point

$$
3 - 3x^2 = 0
$$

$$
x^2 = 1 \Rightarrow x = 1
$$

(Positive value is used for dimensions.)

### Step 4: Find $y$

$$
y = 3 - 1^2 = 2
$$

### Step 5: Maximum area

$$
A = 1 \cdot 2 = 2
$$

### Result

- Dimensions: $x = 1$, $y = 2$
- Maximum area: $A = 2$
