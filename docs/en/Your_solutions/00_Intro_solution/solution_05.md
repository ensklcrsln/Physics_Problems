## 5. Trigonometry

Given:
- Magnitude: $15$
- Angle: $60^\circ$

### Step 1: Resolve into components

$$
A_x = 15 \cos(60^\circ)
$$

$$
A_y = 15 \sin(60^\circ)
$$

### Step 2: Substitute known values

$$
\cos(60^\circ) = \frac{1}{2}, \quad \sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

$$
A_x = 15 \cdot \frac{1}{2} = 7.5
$$

$$
A_y = 15 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = \frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 12.99
$$

### Result

$$
(A_x, A_y) = (7.5, 12.99)
$$
