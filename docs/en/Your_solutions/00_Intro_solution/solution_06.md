## 6. Function Analysis

Given:

$$
f(x) = 3x^2 - 12x + 7
$$

### Step 1: First derivative

$$
f'(x) = 6x - 12
$$

### Step 2: Find critical point

$$
6x - 12 = 0
$$

$$
x = 2
$$

### Step 3: Second derivative

$$
f''(x) = 6
$$

Since $f''(x) > 0$, the function has a minimum.

### Step 4: Find function value

$$
f(2) = 3(2^2) - 12(2) + 7
$$

$$
f(2) = 12 - 24 + 7 = -5
$$

### Result

Minimum point:

$$
(2, -5)
$$
