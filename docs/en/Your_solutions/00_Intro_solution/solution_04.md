## 4. Rearranging Formulas

Given:

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

### Step 1: Eliminate the square root

Square both sides:

$$
T^2 = (2\pi)^2 \cdot \frac{L}{g}
$$

### Step 2: Simplify

$$
(2\pi)^2 = 4\pi^2
$$

So:

$$
T^2 = 4\pi^2 \frac{L}{g}
$$

### Step 3: Multiply both sides by $g$

$$
gT^2 = 4\pi^2 L
$$

### Step 4: Solve for $g$

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}
$$

### Result

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}
$$
