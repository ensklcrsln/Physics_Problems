## 3. Proportionality (Gravity)

**Law:**

$$
F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

### Step 1: Apply the changes

Given:
- $r' = 2r$
- $m_1' = 0.5m_1$
- $m_2' = 0.5m_2$

Substitute into the formula:

$$
F' = G \frac{m_1' m_2'}{(r')^2}
$$

### Step 2: Replace variables

$$
F' = G \frac{(0.5m_1)(0.5m_2)}{(2r)^2}
$$

### Step 3: Simplify step by step

$$
(0.5)(0.5) = 0.25
$$

$$
(2r)^2 = 4r^2
$$

So:

$$
F' = G \frac{0.25 m_1 m_2}{4r^2}
$$

### Step 4: Final simplification

$$
F' = \frac{0.25}{4} \cdot G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

$$
F' = \frac{1}{16} F
$$

### Result

The force becomes:

$$
F' = \frac{1}{16} F
$$

**Conclusion:** The force decreases by a factor of **16**.
