## 8. Definite Integrals

### Step 1: Integral

$$
\int_{0}^{\pi} \sin(x)\,dx
$$

### Step 2: Antiderivative

$$
\int \sin(x)\,dx = -\cos(x)
$$

### Step 3: Apply limits

$$
[-\cos(x)]_{0}^{\pi}
$$

$$
= -\cos(\pi) + \cos(0)
$$

### Step 4: Substitute values

$$
\cos(\pi) = -1, \quad \cos(0) = 1
$$

$$
= -(-1) + 1 = 1 + 1 = 2
$$

### Result

$$
\int_{0}^{\pi} \sin(x)\,dx = 2
$$
