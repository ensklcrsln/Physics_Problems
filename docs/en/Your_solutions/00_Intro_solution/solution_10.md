## 10. Infinite Series (Ant's Position)

We analyze the motion of the ant using two separate infinite series for the x and y coordinates.

---

### Step 1: X-coordinate series

The x-direction movement is given by:

x = 1 − 1/3 + 1/5 − 1/7 + 1/9 − ...

This is a known alternating series called the Leibniz series.

Its value is:

x = π / 4

So:

x ≈ 0.785

---

### Step 2: Y-coordinate series

The y-direction movement is:

y = 1/2 − 1/4 + 1/6 − 1/8 + 1/10 − ...

We factor out 1/2:

y = (1/2)(1 − 1/2 + 1/3 − 1/4 + 1/5 − ...)

The series inside the parentheses is a known alternating harmonic series:

1 − 1/2 + 1/3 − 1/4 + ... = ln(2)

So:

y = (1/2) × ln(2)

y ≈ 0.346

---

### Step 3: Final position

The final coordinates of the ant are:

(x, y) = (π / 4, (1/2) ln(2))

Approximate values:

(x, y) ≈ (0.785, 0.346)
