## 2. Harmonic Motion

Given:

$$
x(t) = 0.2 \cos(10\pi t)
$$

---

### Step 1: Angular Frequency

$$
\omega = 10\pi
$$

**Detailed Explanation:**
- The angular frequency $\omega$ determines how fast the oscillation occurs in time.
- A larger $\omega$ means more oscillations per second.
- It is directly related to physical properties like mass and spring stiffness.

---

### Step 2: Spring Constant

$$
\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \Rightarrow k = m\omega^2
$$

$$
k = 10 \cdot (10\pi)^2 = 1000\pi^2 \approx 9870 \text{ N/m}
$$

**Detailed Explanation:**
- This relationship comes from Newton’s Second Law applied to a spring system.
- A larger spring constant $k$ means a stiffer spring.
- The stiffness determines how strongly the system resists displacement.
- The higher the stiffness, the faster the oscillation.

---

### Step 3: Total Mechanical Energy

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2
$$

$$
E = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 1000\pi^2 \cdot (0.2)^2 = 20\pi^2 \approx 197 \text{ J}
$$

**Detailed Explanation:**
- The amplitude $A$ represents the maximum displacement.
- At maximum displacement, all energy is stored as potential energy.
- At equilibrium, all energy is kinetic.
- The total energy remains constant because no energy is lost.
