## 4. Energy & Momentum

### Given:

- $m_1 = 0.5$ kg  
- $m_2 = 1.5$ kg  
- $h = 3$ m  

---

### Step 1: Find velocity before collision

We use energy conservation:

$$
v = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.81 \cdot 3} \approx 7.67 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

### Step 2: Apply momentum conservation

$$
m_1 v_1 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

$$
0.5 \cdot 7.67 = 2 \cdot v_f
$$

$$
v_f \approx 1.92 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

### Explanation:

- First, gravitational potential energy converts into kinetic energy.
- Then during collision, momentum is conserved.
- Since the blocks stick together, this is an inelastic collision.
