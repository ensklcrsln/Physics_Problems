# 1. Gravitational Dependence

## 📊 Summary

| Quantity | Result |
|----------|--------|
| Moon period | ≈ 9.8 s |
| Length (T=1s) | ≈ 0.25 m |

---

## 🧠 Explanation

For a simple pendulum:

T = 2π sqrt(L / g)

Key idea:

➡ The period does NOT depend on mass  
➡ It depends only on length and gravity  

---

### Moon case

On the Moon:

g_moon = g / 6

Since:

T ∝ 1 / sqrt(g)

We get:

T_moon = 4 × sqrt(6) ≈ 9.8 s

➡ The pendulum swings slower on the Moon  

---

### Length for T = 1 s

Rearrange:

L = g / (4π²)

≈ 0.25 m

➡ Shorter pendulum → faster oscillation
