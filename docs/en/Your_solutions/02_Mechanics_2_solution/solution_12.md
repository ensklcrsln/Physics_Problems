## 12. Work and Energy

### Given:

$$
F = (6, 2), \quad m = 2
$$

---

### Step 1: Acceleration

$$
a = (3, 1)
$$

---

### Step 2: Velocity

$$
v = (1 + 3t,\ -1 + t)
$$

---

### Step 3: Position

$$
x = t + \frac{3}{2}t^2
$$

$$
y = -t + \frac{1}{2}t^2
$$

---

### Step 4: Work at $t=3$

$$
r = (16.5, 1.5)
$$

$$
W = 102 \text{ J}
$$

---

### Step 5: Work-Energy check

$$
\Delta E = 102 \text{ J}
$$

---

### Explanation:

- Work equals change in kinetic energy.
- This confirms the Work-Energy Theorem.
