# Section 1: Mechanics I — Detailed Explanations

---

## 1. Projectile Motion

### 🎯 Problem understanding

A projectile is launched with:
- Initial velocity = 100 m/s
- Angle = 37°
- No air resistance

We split motion into two independent directions:
- Horizontal (x-direction)
- Vertical (y-direction)

---

## 🧠 Why do we split into components?

We use splitting because:

👉 Motion in 2D is easier to analyze as two 1D problems  
👉 Gravity only affects vertical motion  
👉 Horizontal motion has constant velocity (no acceleration)

So we break velocity into:
- x-component
- y-component

---

## 📌 Step 1: Velocity components

We resolve the velocity:

- Horizontal velocity:
  v_x = v_0 × cos(37°)

- Vertical velocity:
  v_y = v_0 × sin(37°)

This is done because:
👉 cosine gives horizontal projection  
👉 sine gives vertical projection  

---

## 📌 Step 2: Equations of motion

Horizontal motion:
- No acceleration
- Constant velocity

So:
x(t) = v_x × t

Vertical motion:
- Acceleration = −g (gravity)

So:
y(t) = v_y × t − (1/2) g t²

---

## 📌 Step 3: Time of flight

Time of flight is when projectile returns to ground:

y(t) = 0

So:
t = (2 v_0 sinθ) / g

---

## 📌 Step 4: Maximum height

At maximum height:
vertical velocity = 0

So:
H = (v_0² sin²θ) / (2g)

---

## 📌 Step 5: Range

Range is total horizontal distance:

R = horizontal velocity × total time

So:
R = (v_0² sin(2θ)) / g
